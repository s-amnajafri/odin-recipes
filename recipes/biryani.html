Biryani

A delicious plate of homemade biryani

Biryani is a fragrant, celebratory South Asian mixed rice dish, originating from Persian and Mughal cuisine. Traditionally made using the dum pukht method, where the pot is sealed with dough to steam-cook the ingredients together, allowing the rice to absorb the meat juices.

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice 3 cups
  • Chicken 1 kg
  • Yoghurt 1 cup
  • Ginger Garlic Paste 2 tsp
  • Lemon Juice 2-4 tsp
  • Onions 2-3
  • Tomatoes 2-3
  • Green Chillies 2
  • Coriander & Mint Leaves
  • Cloves 3-4
  • Oil 1/2 cup

Steps:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Mix chicken with yogurt, garlic-ginger paste, and biryani spices (like Shan Food mix or homemade spices). Let it sit for 2 hours or overnight for best results.
  2. Fry Onions & Make Gravy: Heat oil and fry sliced onions until golden brown; remove half for topping. Add chicken, tomatoes, and yogurt to the remaining onions in the pan, cooking until the oil separates.
  3. Boil Rice: Boil 2 liters of water with salt, bay leaves, cinnamon, and cumin. Cook soaked rice until 70-80% done (it should have a slight bite). Drain thoroughly.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In a heavy-bottom pot, add a layer of gravy, followed by half the rice, then the remaining chicken, and finally the rest of the rice.
  5. Add Toppings & Steam (Dum): Top with fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron water/kewra water. Seal the lid tightly (with dough or foil) and cook on very low heat for 20-30 minutes.
  6. Serve: Let it rest for 10 minutes before gently mixing and serving.
